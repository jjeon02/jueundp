Korean Gul-ja

Gulja is a unit that does not exist in English. To make a word or sentence, gul-ja is necessary.


g/k + a = ga/ka

consonant + vowel


The most basic structure. This is the minimum structure that is required in order to make a word

g + a + k = gak

consonant + vowel + consonant

g + a + l/r + k = gak

consonant + vowel + consonants


Consonant sound different depend on their location, and sometimes they're not pronounced. The maximum number of con-boxsonants that can be at the end of gul-ja is two.

gg + a = gga

consonants + vowel


Double consonants can be at the front

gg + a + n = ggan

consonants + vowel + consonant

gg + a + l/r + k = ggak

consonants + vowel + consonants


Similar rule applies.

g + wae = gwae

consonant + vowel

Multiple-vowel structure can be also used.

g + wae + n = gwaen

consonant + vowel + consonant

g + wae + n + h = gwaen

consonant + vowel + consonants

Similar rule applies.


Korean Word

같

One Gulja

The minimum element that makes word

각도

Multiple Gulja

Gulja can be combined and create either similar or different meaning.

블라디보스토크

Long Word

There are no specific limit of gulja on one word but most of the time it is common for one word to have less than 10 gulja.

같다

Multiple Gulja

Every sentence need a grammatic gulja that function as finishing the sentence. In casual con-boxvertaion, this rule often does not apply.


Korean Sentence

Korean Language have been influenced from different cultures including ones from China, Japan and European countries and North America. Chinese-charactor based words take big portion of Korean. After colonization, Korean language was highly influenced by Japanese and English.


나는 강에서
라디오를 들었다
(I listened to the Radio at River)

Chinese

강(river)

Korean

나(me),듣(listen)

Korean Grammar Component

는,에서,를,다

Foreign Language

라디오(radio)
